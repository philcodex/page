In the shift toward remote work, the "meeting" has often been unfairly maligned as a productivity killer. While poorly structured calendar invites are indeed draining, we often overlook the essential human element that meetings provide. In a digital-first environment, meetings shouldn't just be about status updates—they are the heartbeat of team spirit.

### The Purpose of Presence
Without the physical "watercooler," remote teams risk becoming a collection of isolated silos. Regular, synchronous meetings serve as a vital anchor for morale. They remind us that there are real humans behind the avatars. When we see a colleague’s face or hear their laugh, we build the psychological safety necessary for high-level collaboration.

### Making Them Work Better
To make these sessions work better, we must separate **transactional** meetings from **relational** ones. 

* **Keep it Regular:** Predictability reduces anxiety and ensures no one feels "out of the loop."
* **Prioritize Connection:** Dedicate the first five minutes to non-work updates. 
* **Purpose-Driven:** If it’s just a status report, use Slack. If it requires brainstorming or bonding, get on a call.

By reframing meetings as a tool for connection rather than just coordination, remote teams can maintain high morale and a thriving culture.
